Protecting Health Information is Diya Health’s Top Priority

     

Threat to Privacy

Recent growth in the use of telehealth makes it more difficult to keep patients' medical information safe. As more private practices and hospitals adopt technologies for telehealth and remote patient monitoring — in part because of the pandemic — the point-of-entry for hackers widens.

Stringent protection of privacy is an essential element of good medical-record software. Diya Health's products, DiyaMD and myDiya, meet the highest standards for security.

Diya Health's Security Standards

Diya Health's security features close off the point-of-entry for hackers.  They go beyond the standards mandated by the U.S.’s HIPAA and the E.U.’s GDPR requirements:

  • We act like a bank's safe deposit box. We are the repository, but the patient holds the key. We integrate with e-mail services; no user names or passwords are stored on our system.
  • We store health information on the most secure cloud systems available: Microsoft Azure and Amazon Web Services.
  • Ours is an opt-in model: Records owned by clinicians are never shared unless done so explicitly by the clinician.
  • We do not sell data to pharmaceutical companies, insurers, employers, or other businesses.
  • Our employees do not have access to patients’ records — not even our CEO.
  • We maintain a complete audit trail of all information access.

Our products are a robust and secure platform for e-services and management of health information.  But at all times, it is the patient, and the caregivers chosen by the patient, who completely control the health records. We take seriously the protection of privacy, and will uphold the highest security standards while we promote better quality of care.
